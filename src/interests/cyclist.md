Growing up in the Irish countryside, I can't remember a time I didn't enjoy cycling. From that enjoyment, cycling has become an important aspect of my life, allowing me to challenge myslef physically and raise money for some great causes.

<!-- excerpt end -->

When I began studying at NCI in 2012, I immediately signed up for the Cycling Club's Dublin - Galway (200+km) charity cycle.

## At first

Taking on this challenge was a way for me to challenge myself physically, helping me to keep a good balance while I began my 3rd level education. I also decided to challenge myself with restoring my dad's 1987 Raleigh racing bike to use for this cycle. I completed this cycle, raising money for the Central Remedial Clinic in Dublin.

## Since then

Since then I have challenged myself to numerous charity cycles. These include Tour de Kilkenny, The Leinster Loop & Westport to Achill. There will no doubt be several more completed in the future. While I usually use something more modern these days, my vintage 1987 racing bike is still used regularly.
